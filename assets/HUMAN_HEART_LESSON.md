# The Human Heart: Anatomy and Physiology

## Introduction

The **human heart** is a muscular, four-chambered organ that pumps blood throughout the body. Located in the mediastinum (between the lungs), the heart is approximately the size of a closed fist and weighs about 250-350 grams (8.8-12.3 ounces) in adults. It is the primary organ of the circulatory system and works continuously without rest to deliver oxygen-rich blood to all tissues and organs.

**Key Facts:**
- **Weight:** 250-350 grams
- **Size:** About the size of a fist
- **Location:** Left side of the chest, protected by the rib cage
- **Beats per minute:** 60-100 (at rest)
- **Total beats per day:** Approximately 100,000
- **Lifespan function:** Beats continuously for 70-80+ years

---

## Heart Overview

### Structure and Location

The heart is a hollow, muscular organ divided into four chambers:
- **Upper chambers (Atria):** Right and left atria receive blood
- **Lower chambers (Ventricles):** Right and left ventricles pump blood

### The Three Layers

**Epicardium (Visceral Pericardium)**
- Outer layer in direct contact with the heart
- Part of the serous pericardium
- Smooth membrane reducing friction

**Myocardium (Cardiac Muscle)**
- Thick, muscular middle layer
- Responsible for the heart's contractions
- Composed of cardiac muscle cells (cardiomyocytes)
- Striated muscle unique to the heart

**Endocardium (Endothelium)**
- Inner lining of the heart chambers
- Smooth surface reduces friction with blood
- Contains conduction system components

### External Structure

**Pericardium**
- Double-walled sac surrounding the heart
- Provides protection and reduces friction
- Visceral pericardium (inner) and parietal pericardium (outer)
- Contains small amount of lubricating fluid

**Coronary Arteries**
- Supply oxygen-rich blood to the heart muscle itself
- Left coronary artery and right coronary artery
- Critical for heart health and function

---

## Heart Structure

### The Four Chambers

**Right Atrium**
- Receives deoxygenated blood from the body
- Receives blood via superior and inferior vena cavae
- Thin-walled, low-pressure chamber
- Contracts to push blood into the right ventricle

**Right Ventricle**
- Pumps blood to the lungs
- Sends deoxygenated blood via the pulmonary artery
- Thinner walls than the left ventricle
- Develops less pressure than left ventricle

**Left Atrium**
- Receives oxygenated blood from the lungs
- Receives blood via four pulmonary veins
- Thin-walled chamber
- Contracts to push blood into the left ventricle

**Left Ventricle**
- Pumps oxygenated blood to the entire body
- Sends blood via the aorta
- Thickest and most muscular chamber
- Generates highest pressure (systolic pressure)

### The Septum

The **cardiac septum** divides the heart into left and right sides:
- **Atrial septum:** Separates the two atria
- **Ventricular septum:** Separates the two ventricles
- **Foramen ovale:** Opening in fetal heart (normally closes at birth)

---

## Heart Valves

### Valve Function

Heart valves maintain unidirectional blood flow and prevent backflow.

**Atrioventricular Valves (AV Valves)**
- Located between atria and ventricles
- Prevent blood from flowing backward into the atria

**Semilunar Valves**
- Located between ventricles and arteries
- Prevent blood from flowing back into the ventricles

### The Four Valves

**Tricuspid Valve**
- Between right atrium and right ventricle
- Has three cusps (leaflets)
- Prevents backflow into right atrium

**Mitral Valve (Bicuspid Valve)**
- Between left atrium and left ventricle
- Has two cusps
- Most commonly affected by heart disease

**Pulmonary Valve (Pulmonic Valve)**
- Between right ventricle and pulmonary artery
- Semilunar valve structure
- Prevents backflow into right ventricle

**Aortic Valve**
- Between left ventricle and aorta
- Semilunar valve structure
- Prevents backflow into left ventricle

### Heart Sounds

- **Lubb:** Closing of atrioventricular valves (S1)
- **Dupp:** Closing of semilunar valves (S2)
- "Lubb-dupp" pattern repeats with each heartbeat

---

## Blood Flow Through the Heart

### Systemic and Pulmonary Circulation

**Step-by-step blood flow:**

1. **Deoxygenated blood enters** the right atrium from the vena cava
2. **Right atrium contracts**, pushing blood through the tricuspid valve
3. **Blood fills** the right ventricle
4. **Right ventricle contracts**, pushing blood through the pulmonary valve
5. **Blood enters** the pulmonary artery to the lungs
6. **Oxygenated blood returns** from lungs via pulmonary veins
7. **Blood enters** the left atrium
8. **Left atrium contracts**, pushing blood through the mitral valve
9. **Blood fills** the left ventricle
10. **Left ventricle contracts** with maximum force
11. **Blood is ejected** through the aortic valve into the aorta
12. **Oxygenated blood** distributes throughout the body

**Important fact:** Oxygen-poor and oxygen-rich blood never mix due to the cardiac septum.

## The Cardiac Cycle

### Phases of the Heartbeat

**Atrial Systole**
- Duration: About 0.1 seconds
- Atria contract simultaneously
- Blood moves into ventricles
- Ventricles are in diastole (relaxed)

**Isovolumetric Ventricular Contraction**
- Ventricles begin contracting
- All four valves are closed
- Ventricular pressure increases rapidly
- No change in chamber volume

**Ventricular Ejection**
- Ventricles contract forcefully
- Semilunar valves open
- Blood is pumped into arteries
- About two-thirds of blood is ejected

**Isovolumetric Ventricular Relaxation**
- Ventricles begin to relax
- Atrioventricular valves close
- Semilunar valves close
- All four valves are momentarily closed

**Ventricular Filling (Diastole)**
- Ventricles relax completely
- Atrioventricular valves open
- Blood flows passively into ventricles
- Represents most of the cycle duration

### Duration and Heart Rate

- **Complete cycle:** One heartbeat
- **At rest:** About 0.8 seconds
- **Heart rate:** 60-100 beats per minute (normal adult)
- **Percentage of time:** Ventricles spend 2/3 of cycle relaxing

## Conduction System

The heart has its own electrical system:

### Sinoatrial (SA) Node
- Natural pacemaker of the heart
- Located in the right atrium
- Generates electrical impulses
- Rate: 60-100 beats per minute at rest

### Atrioventricular (AV) Node
- Located in the right atrium near the ventricles
- Delays signal to allow atrial contraction
- Transmits signal to ventricles

### Bundle of His
- Conducts signal from AV node
- Divides into left and right bundle branches

### Purkinje Fibers
- Transmit signal throughout the ventricles
- Cause coordinated ventricular contraction

---

## Coronary Circulation

### The Coronary Arteries

**Left Coronary Artery**
- Supplies left ventricle and left atrium
- Divides into left anterior descending and left circumflex
- Accounts for ~70% of blood supply

**Right Coronary Artery**
- Supplies right ventricle and right atrium
- Often supplies the SA and AV nodes
- Accounts for ~30% of blood supply

### Coronary Veins
- **Great cardiac vein:** Drains left ventricle
- **Middle cardiac vein:** Drains right ventricle
- **Small cardiac vein:** Drains right ventricle and right atrium
- **Coronary sinus:** Collects all coronary veins

---

## Heart Health and Disease

### Cardiac Output

**Cardiac output = Stroke volume √ó Heart rate**

- **Stroke volume:** Amount of blood pumped per beat (about 70 mL)
- **Cardiac output:** About 5 liters per minute at rest
- **Can increase:** 5-fold during intense exercise

### Common Heart Conditions

**Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)**
- Plaque buildup in coronary arteries
- Reduces blood flow to heart muscle
- Leading cause of heart attack

**Heart Failure**
- Heart cannot pump enough blood
- Can affect left, right, or both sides
- Causes fluid accumulation

**Arrhythmia**
- Abnormal heart rhythm
- Can be fast (tachycardia) or slow (bradycardia)
- May require intervention

**Heart Valve Disease**
- Stenosis: Valve doesn't open completely
- Regurgitation: Valve doesn't close completely
- Can cause blood to flow backward

### Heart Health Tips

1. **Exercise regularly:** 150 minutes moderate activity per week
2. **Eat healthy:** Low in salt, saturated fat, and cholesterol
3. **Maintain weight:** Obesity increases heart disease risk
4. **Don't smoke:** Smoking damages heart and blood vessels
5. **Manage stress:** Chronic stress affects heart health
6. **Get enough sleep:** 7-9 hours per night
7. **Control blood pressure:** High blood pressure damages the heart
8. **Limit alcohol:** Excessive drinking weakens the heart

## Fascinating Heart Facts

- **Heart beats:** 100,000 times per day
- **Blood pumped:** 2,000 gallons per day
- **Pressure:** Left ventricle generates ~120 mmHg systolic pressure
- **Sound transmission:** Heart sounds can be heard with a stethoscope
- **Size comparison:** Heart is about the size of a closed fist
- **Temperature:** Heart generates heat to keep the body warm
- **Embryonic origin:** Forms from mesoderm in first 3 weeks
- **Unique feature:** Only organ that can beat outside the body

## Heart Overview

### Basic Facts About the Heart

- **Size:** Approximately the size of a closed fist (about 12 cm long)
- **Weight:** 250-350 grams (8-12 ounces)
- **Location:** In the thoracic cavity, slightly left of center
- **Function:** Pumps blood to the lungs and entire body
- **Heart Rate:** 60-100 beats per minute at rest
- **Lifespan:** Beats continuously from before birth until death

---

## Heart Structure

### The Three Layers

**Epicardium (Outer Layer):**
- Thin, protective outer layer
- Produces lubricating fluid
- Reduces friction during heartbeats

**Myocardium (Middle Layer):**
- Thick muscular layer
- Contracts to pump blood
- Composed of cardiac muscle cells
- Most powerful part of the heart

**Endocardium (Inner Layer):**
- Smooth inner lining
- Reduces friction for blood flow
- Continuous with blood vessel linings

### The Pericardium

- Double-walled membrane sac surrounding the heart
- Parietal pericardium (outer layer)
- Visceral pericardium (inner layer)
- Contains lubricating fluid for smooth movement

---

## The Four Chambers

### Right Atrium
- Receives deoxygenated blood from the body
- Blood enters via superior and inferior vena cava
- Thin-walled, receives blood passively

### Right Ventricle
- Pumps deoxygenated blood to the lungs
- Thicker muscular walls than atria
- Sends blood via pulmonary artery

### Left Atrium
- Receives oxygen-rich blood from the lungs
- Blood enters via four pulmonary veins
- Thin-walled, smaller than right atrium

### Left Ventricle
- Pumps oxygen-rich blood to the entire body
- Thick muscular walls (thickest heart chamber)
- Most powerful chamber
- Sends blood via aorta

---

## Heart Valves

### Four Essential Valves

**Tricuspid Valve:**
- Location: Between right atrium and right ventricle
- Function: Prevents backflow of blood
- Three leaflets/cusps

**Pulmonary Valve:**
- Location: Between right ventricle and pulmonary artery
- Function: Prevents blood return to right ventricle
- Two to three leaflets

**Mitral Valve (Bicuspid Valve):**
- Location: Between left atrium and left ventricle
- Function: Prevents backflow into left atrium
- Two leaflets

**Aortic Valve:**
- Location: Between left ventricle and aorta
- Function: Prevents blood return to left ventricle
- Three leaflets
- Highest pressure valve

### Valve Function

Valves open and close in response to pressure changes:
- When pressure increases on one side, the valve opens
- When pressure reverses, the valve closes
- This prevents backward blood flow

---

## Blood Flow

### The Complete Circulation Path

1. **Deoxygenated blood** enters the right atrium
2. Blood flows through tricuspid valve to right ventricle
3. Right ventricle pumps blood through pulmonary valve to lungs
4. **Oxygen-rich blood** returns from lungs to left atrium
5. Blood flows through mitral valve to left ventricle
6. Left ventricle pumps blood through aortic valve to aorta
7. Blood circulates to the entire body
8. Process repeats

### Major Blood Vessels

- **Superior Vena Cava:** Brings blood from upper body
- **Inferior Vena Cava:** Brings blood from lower body
- **Pulmonary Artery:** Carries deoxygenated blood to lungs
- **Pulmonary Veins:** Return oxygenated blood from lungs
- **Aorta:** Major artery carrying blood to the body

---

## The Cardiac Cycle

### Systole (Contraction)

**Atrial Systole:**
- Atria contract
- Pushes blood into ventricles
- Lasts about 0.1 seconds

**Ventricular Systole:**
- Ventricles contract
- Pumps blood into arteries
- Lasts about 0.3 seconds

### Diastole (Relaxation)

- Heart chambers relax and fill with blood
- Lasts about 0.5 seconds
- Allows blood to enter from veins

### The Heartbeat Sound

- **"Lub"** = Tricuspid and mitral valves closing
- **"Dub"** = Pulmonary and aortic valves closing

## Heart Health

### Keeping Your Heart Healthy

**Cardiovascular Risk Factors:**
- High blood pressure
- High cholesterol
- Smoking
- Obesity
- Diabetes
- Physical inactivity
- Stress

**Healthy Heart Habits:**
- Regular exercise (150 minutes per week)
- Balanced diet (low salt, high fiber)
- Maintain healthy weight
- Manage stress
- Get adequate sleep (7-9 hours)
- Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol
- Regular health checkups

### Common Heart Conditions

- **Arrhythmia:** Irregular heartbeat
- **Coronary Artery Disease:** Plaque buildup in arteries
- **Heart Attack:** Blood clot stops blood flow
- **Heart Failure:** Heart can't pump enough blood
- **Valve Disease:** Valves don't work properly

---

## Interesting Facts

- Your heart pumps about 2,000 gallons of blood daily
- Heart rate increases during exercise and stress
- Women's hearts typically beat faster than men's
- The left ventricle can create enough pressure to squirt blood 30 feet
- A baby's heart beats faster (120-160 beats per minute) than an adult's
- Heart disease is the leading cause of death worldwide
